Nablus City

Nablus, a historic city in the northern West Bank, Palestine, is known for its rich cultural and historical significance.
Founded in 72 CE by the Romans, it has a long heritage reflected in its old markets, Ottoman-era architecture,
and soap-making industry. Nablus is also renowned for its sweet delicacies, particularly knafeh.
The city serves as an economic and cultural hub, and despite the challenges it has faced due to political tensions,
it remains a symbol of Palestinian resilience and tradition.
